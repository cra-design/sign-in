Who can register for a CRA account

You can register for a CRA account if you have:

  • A social insurance number (SIN)
  • A filed and assessed income tax and benefit return from the current or previous tax year

What you can access

After you sign in to your CRA account, you can use any of the following:

My Account
View and manage your personal and tax information, apply for benefits, and make payments to the CRA.
Read more: About My Account
My Business Account
View and update your business tax information, and file and submit returns for many programs.
Read more: About My Business Account
Represent a Client
Manage business, trust, or individual information on behalf of someone else with their authorization, including a friend, family member or client.
Read more: About Represent a Client
Non-Resident Withholding Tax
View and manage the non-resident tax account you use for Part XIII withholdings on income paid to non-residents.
Read more: About Non-Resident Withholding Tax

For details about our hours of service and browser requirements, go to CRA account availability.

Registration and sign-in options

There are 3 options for registering for and signing in to your CRA account:

CRA user ID and password
This option lets you register and sign in with a CRA user ID and password.
Learn more: About CRA user ID and password
Sign-In Partner
This option lets you register and sign in with your online banking information.
Learn more: About Sign-In Partners
Provincial partner
This option lets you register and sign in with a BC Services Card or an Alberta.ca account.
Learn more: About provincial partners

Privacy commitments

The CRA does not share your registration information with any third party. The information you provide is held by the CRA and is protected under the federal Privacy Act.
